Overview
Welcome to the first European School of Information Theory Data-to-Information Competition, organized by the IEEE Information Theory Society Student and Outreach Committee. The challenge asks participants to chart their way through noisy, partial, and imperfect wireless-channel measurements using real-world Channel State Information data, with the final goal of performing CSI-based localization.

This Kaggle page hosts Task 2 — Unlearning and Data Reliability, the advanced challenge of ESIT-D2I 2026. Participants start from a pretrained localization model whose training set included a subset of users with corrupted trajectories, and must efficiently remove the influence of those users — without retraining from scratch — once their identities are partially revealed.

The competition includes a warm-up on CSI localization basics, a main challenge on learning under noisy conditions, and an advanced challenge on unlearning and data reliability.

Participants will use data based on the DICHASUS Dataset collected at the Arena2036 research campus, including labeled CSI/location/timestamp measurements and unlabeled CSI/timestamp measurements.

Note: This Kaggle competition represents the second half of the ESIT-D2I 2026: Charting the Path to Bifröst competition. The other half is Task 1 ESIT-D2I 2026: Charting the Path to Bifröst, devoted to learning robust localization models directly under noisy conditions.
