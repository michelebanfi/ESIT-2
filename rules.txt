9. OVERALL RANKING AND PRIZE DETERMINATION (ESIT-D2I 2026)
a. Two-task structure. ESIT-D2I 2026 consists of two separate Kaggle competitions — one for Task 1 and one for Task 2 — each with its own evaluation metric and Kaggle leaderboard. Prizes are awarded based on combined performance across both tasks, not on the leaderboard of any single task in isolation.

b. Combined ranking. For each Team that has submitted to both tasks, the Competition Sponsor will compute the final overall ranking as follows:

Determine each Team's final rank on the private leaderboard of Task 1 (call it R₁) and on the private leaderboard of Task 2 (call it R₂).

The Team's combined score is R₁ + R₂ (lower is better).

Teams are ranked by their combined score in ascending order.

c. Tie-breaking. In the event that two or more Teams have the same combined score, ties are broken using the average normalized relative difference to the best Team in each task. Specifically, for each tied Team i and each task t ∈ {1, 2}, let s_{i,t} denote the Team's private-leaderboard score on task t, and let s*_t denote the best (winning) private-leaderboard score on task t. The normalized relative difference is defined as

δ_{i,t} = (s_{i,t} − s*_t) / |s*_t|

(or, if s*_t = 0, δ_{i,t} = s_{i,t} − s*_t). The Team's tie-breaking statistic is the mean over the two tasks, δ_i = (δ_{i,1} + δ_{i,2}) / 2. Lower δ_i wins. If a tie persists after this procedure, the Competition Sponsor will resolve the tie at its sole discretion.

d. Eligibility for the overall ranking. Only Teams that (i) submit a valid scored entry to both Task 1 and Task 2, (ii) maintain a consistent Team composition across the two tasks (Section 2.1.f), and (iii) include at least one ESIT 2026 participant (Section 2.7.b) are eligible for the overall prize ranking.

e. Sponsor discretion. The Competition Sponsor reserves the right to:

modify or refine the combined-ranking procedure or tie-breaking rule if, prior to the close of the Competition, an unforeseen issue makes the above procedure infeasible or manifestly unfair;

disqualify any Team from the overall ranking for violation of these Rules, including but not limited to suspected collusion, prohibited use of External Data or hidden labels, multi-account submissions, or failure to meet the reproducibility requirements of Section 2.8;

decline to award any or all prizes if no eligible Team meets a minimum quality threshold (e.g., no Team beats a non-trivial baseline), at the Sponsor's sole discretion.

f. Final and binding. The decisions of the Competition Sponsor regarding the overall ranking, tie-breaking, eligibility, and prize awards are final and binding.
